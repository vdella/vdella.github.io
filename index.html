about

I'm an MSc student in Automation & Systems Engineering at UFSC with a Computer Science background, working where energy systems meet software.

My dissertation is a digital-twin component for a microgrid — a 5-cell lithium battery bank you can observe, forecast, and reason about in real time. I write Python every day, keep C++ sharp for the control-loop work, and I'm building toward Go and Rust.

The thread through all of it: take a messy physical system, give it a faithful software shadow, and make that shadow useful.

microgrid digital twin 01 / 03

BVOLT

A digital twin for a lithium battery bank, built on a hexagonal ports-and-adapters architecture so the physics, the data, and the interfaces stay cleanly separable. A Temporal Fusion Transformer forecasts state-of-charge, while a Modbus → InfluxDB → Grafana pipeline streams live telemetry from cell to dashboard.
